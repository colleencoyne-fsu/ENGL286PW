    Section Policies

    Subsection Attendance

    While I do take attendance, there is no separate attendance grade. You should plan to attend all class meetings, and it’s essential for you to be active on Canvas as well. In the event that you are faced with extreme, extenuating circumstances (e.g., prolonged serious illness, death in the immediate family, jury duty, military service, etc.), you should contact the Dean of Students Office, which will help you document an extended absence.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Communication

    I welcome your questions and comments via email (ccoyne1@framingham.edu), which I’ll typically check and respond to between 9:30 a.m. and 5:30 p.m., Monday through Friday (excluding holidays—see the Academic Calendar). Please allow up to 24 hours for a response, and feel free to follow up if you don’t receive a response within this time. Check your email regularly for instructor communications and course announcements, which will also appear on Canvas. I’ll provide grades and written comments on assignments through Canvas only, not over email. For in-depth conversations (e.g., class performance, feedback on assignments, etc.), please schedule an appointment or drop in during Student Hours).
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Recording Classes

    This class may use video and audio recordings of faculty and students, both online and in person, to better support learning in a blended format. By enrolling in the course, students are consenting to being recorded in this class and may only withdraw such consent by informing the course instructor in writing. As these recordings may contain intellectual property as well as confidential student information (e.g., student names, likenesses), sharing or transferring recordings of such content by any method currently available or any method that may become available in the future is not allowed, and copies of such recordings should not be provided to others; uploaded, linked, embedded, or otherwise posted via file-sharing, social media, or other sites or services which could enable anyone to view or hear them who is not currently enrolled in the course; or share them in any other way. Access to video and audio recordings in this class is for personal educational use only and is available only to individuals currently enrolled in the class, unless faculty permission is expressly granted. Recording and/or sharing course materials, including video and audio files, without the written consent of the course instructor is a violation of University policies (e.g., academic honesty) and will be reported to the College Dean for further action and/or discipline.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Academic Honesty

    All the work you turn in for this class (and beyond) must be your own. Scholastic dishonesty includes, but is not limited to (1) plagiarism, defined as claiming as one’s own work the published or unpublished literal or paraphrased work of another; (2) unauthorized collaboration with other individuals in the preparation of course assignments; and (3) submitting without authorization the same assignment for credit in more than one course. Submitting scholastically dishonest work is grounds for failing the assignment/course. See the full University Academic Honesty Policy in the current Undergraduate Catalog. If you’re struggling with an assignment, please meet with me, and take advantage of the services that CASA offers (see below).
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Use of Generative AI

    Generative artificial intelligence (GAI) tools—software that creates new text, images, computer code, audio, video, and other content—have become widely available. Well-known examples include ChatGPT for text and DALL•E for images. This policy governs all such tools, including those released during our semester together. When noted in the assignment description, you may use these tools to help you with assignments in the specific ways indicated.
    🔗
    Remember that AI tools are typically trained on limited datasets that may be out of date and include copyrighted material; therefore, relying on a generative AI tool may result in plagiarism or copyright violations. Also, keep in mind that the goal of generative AI tools is to produce content that seems to have been produced by a human, not to produce accurate or reliable content; therefore, relying on a generative AI tool may result in your submission of inaccurate content. It is your responsibility—not the tool’s—to assure the quality and integrity of the work you submit in any college course. Finally, using generative AI erases you as the writer and silences your voice—and your voice is important!
    🔗
    If you use generative AI tools to complete assignments in this course in ways that I have not explicitly authorized, you will be in violation of Framingham State’s Academic Honesty policy. Please act with integrity, for the sake of both your personal character and your academic record.

    Subsection Disability/Access Services

    The University is committed to making reasonable modifications and accommodations to ensure that its programs and activities do not discriminate, or have the effect of discriminating, on the basis of disability. The Disability/Access Services Office serves students with learning and psychiatric disabilities, as well as students with visual, mobility, and hearing impairments. For further information, please contact Dr. LaDonna Bridges, Dean of Student Success and Persistence, at 508-626-4906 or lbridges@framingham.edu.

    Subsection Antiracism at FSU

    At Framingham State University, faculty, staff, and students work together to sustain a learning, working, and living community free from hate, discrimination, harassment, and intolerance. We recognize the damaging effects of systemic racism—including policies, structures, and historic practices—on the experience and success of communities of color. Coming from different backgrounds and different levels of privilege, we can all affirm and engage in antiracist work.
    🔗
    Diversity of voices, and of minds, strengthens our ability to solve problems and to ask and answer questions about the world we share. As your instructor, I commit to upholding community values of inclusion, civility, accessibility, and mutual respect. I expect this class to commit to creating a community that affirms and welcomes all persons from diverse backgrounds and experiences, and supports the realization of everyone’s potential.
    🔗
    The faculty at Framingham State University are against prejudice, discrimination, and bias of any kind. If you experience or witness an act of bias at Framingham State University, whether committed by a fellow student, staff member, or faculty member, please note that you can report the incident online by completing a bias incident response form. You may file a report anonymously or choose to include your name. The Bias Education Response Team (BERT) reviews and handles these reports.
    🔗
    The English Department supports and advocates for all Framingham State University students, regardless of identity and/or background. Framingham State University values diversity, inclusion, and creating a respectful and supportive learning environment for all members of our community.
    🔗

    Subsection FSU Notice of Non-Discrimination and Diversity

    Framingham State University is committed to a policy of non-discrimination, equal opportunity, diversity, and affirmative action. The University is dedicated to providing educational, working, and living environments that value the diverse backgrounds of all people. Furthermore, the Massachusetts Civil Rights Act (“MCRA,” M.G.L. c. 12, §§ 11H, 11I, 11J) protects the rights of all residents of and visitors to Massachusetts to be free from bias-motivated threats, intimidation, and coercion that interfere with their civil rights. The MCRA protects the right to attend school, live peacefully, and enjoy other basic rights.

    If you’re unable to be physically, mentally, or psychologically available for class because some of your basic needs are not being met, FSU has resources that may help. Please talk to me if you don’t know where to start.

    Subsection Use of Student Work

    Seeing examples of other students’ work can be immensely helpful as you consider the choices you might make in your own work. Occasionally, I’ll share past student work that has successful and unsuccessful components, which we’ll look at together to critically analyze and evaluate. All of this work will be anonymized. Early in the semester, I’ll ask you to fill out an authorization form to allow me to use your work in these and other ways. Please contact me if you have any questions about this.
