    Section Course Overview

    Welcome to Professional Writing at Framingham State University! In this course, you’ll improve your communication skills for college, the workplace, and beyond. We’ll emphasize writing clearly, concisely, and correctly; appropriately using email and other online media for professional messages; and considering effective ways to present your ideas to a variety of audiences. The course includes the writing of a detailed proposal that investigates a real issue affecting the university community.
    🔗

    Subsection Course Description

    From the catalog: "A career-oriented course introducing students to a wide variety of writing formats used in business, government, and the professions. Assignments include resumes and other employment documents, letters and memos, reports, and presentations. This course emphasizes drafting, critiquing, rewriting, and editing, as well as collaborative writing and presentation skills. Prerequisite: ENWR 110 Composition II or permission of the instructor."
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Course Goals and Objectives

    At the conclusion of this course, you will be able to do the following:
    🔗
    • understand and identify contexts, audiences, and goals for various professional writing situations.
      🔗
    • produce work in several genres of professional communications.
      🔗
    • conduct relevant research and strategically communicate findings.
      🔗
    • optimize material for effective visuals, including through charts, graphs, and other media.
      🔗
    • recognize and use language appropriate for workplace environments.
      🔗
    • qualitatively evaluate and respond critically to others’ work, including that of your peers.
      🔗
    • develop a portfolio of professional writing suitable for job applications (including writing samples).
      🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Class Environment

    Writers at all stages always have something to learn from others, and from others’ texts, and we all must always be respectful of one another. One goal of this class is to elevate the level of everyone’s writing, not simply to praise or critique it.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Student Hours (also called Office Hours)

    I’ve set aside this time specifically for you: to answer your questions, discuss your ideas, and talk about any aspect of class, writing, or your college/career plans. My office hours can be either virtual or in person on Mondays and Wednesdays, 11:30 a.m.–12:20 p.m. You may schedule a time on Starfish (login required) or just drop in. I can also meet with you other times by appointment, either virtually or in person.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Texts & Materials

    There is no required textbook to purchase for this course. Instead, you’ll find a selection of readings from open educational resources (OERs) and other content on Canvas. You’ll also watch recorded presentations through Canvas, in which I cover both material from our readings and additional important information about professional writing. These recordings are part of the asynchronous aspect of our course.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Technology

    Canvas is essential for this class, both for submitting assignments and accessing course materials. If you have any problems with Canvas, you may call Canvas Support at (508) 250-0805 or access live chat with Canvas Student Support on the website/in the app. Also, ITS can assist you with other technology issues. You are responsible for familiarizing yourself with the parts of Canvas we’ll use in this class; please don’t hesitate to ask for help if something is unclear.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Coursework and Grading

    On Canvas, I’ll provide detailed assignment descriptions for all Formal Assignments. See below for more on the other components of your grade. Here are the components of your final grade:
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Due Dates / Flexible Deadlines

    Unless otherwise noted, assignments are due by 11:59 p.m. on the day listed in the schedule and on Canvas. I encourage you to adhere to all deadlines to ensure that you stay on track and get the most out of this experience. However, you may need more time to complete an assignment, so you automatically have a one-week grace period. After that, the assignment will close, and you must consult with me to receive an additional extension. It’s your responsibility to be sure you know how to submit each assignment and to confirm that your submission was successful. You should also back up your work so technological failure doesn’t undermine your progress.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Formatting & Submitting Work

    You’ll submit all your assignments through Canvas. For all Formal Assignments and peer reviews, you must attach a file (.doc, .docx, .rtf, or .pdf file only). If you use Google Docs, go to File 🡪 Download as 🡪 to download an appropriate version of your document. Please do not share links to Google Docs, OneDrive, or other online documents unless specifically asked to. If you submit your work in a format I can’t view/read (for example, .pages), I won’t be able to grade it, and you won’t receive credit for the assignment. Your file name should contain your last name and the assignment (for example: “Simpson Correspondence.doc”). If you’re posting to a discussion board, consider creating/saving your post in another program in case you experience connectivity issues.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Discussions

    All the forums listed in the schedule are graded. For all of these, you’ll have the opportunity to post and/or respond to others’ posts in class. If you’re not in class when we’re working on a particular forum, or if you aren’t able to finish the post during class, you’ll have additional time to complete the work for credit. Please see the schedule for forum topics and their associated deadlines; each forum will have a full prompt, including the point value.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Short Reports

    Throughout the semester, you’ll have several opportunities to write short reports to share your progress on a project, provide a recommendation, and fulfill other purposes. Please see the schedule for topics and deadlines. Some reports are worth 3 points, and others are worth 4, for a total of 14 points. Full guidelines are available on Canvas.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Peer Review

    Even though you’ll be working on individual projects during the semester, you won’t be working alone. Professional writing is largely collaborative, and peer review is one way in which people collaborate. You’ll have assigned peer review partners or small groups for each of the three Formal Assignments. If you’re not in class for a peer review session, or if you aren’t able to finish your responses during class, you’ll have additional time to complete the work for credit. Please see the schedule for deadlines and each forum for detailed guidelines. You’ll earn 3 points per Peer Review session for responding to your peers’ work.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Quizzes

    Three short quizzes will check your knowledge on important topics: correspondence, employment materials, and design/visuals. These quizzes, which you’ll take through Canvas, are open book, open note, and open internet (though you should choose your sources wisely). You may take each quiz as many times as you’d like until the deadline (see the schedule). Not taking a quiz will result in earning zero points. I’ll drop your lowest score, and your final quiz grade will be the average of your top two scores, up to 5 points. Please note that the label “Practice Quiz” on Canvas means that the individual quiz score doesn’t count toward your final grade, but quizzes are still graded and are factored into your final grade as described above.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Participation

    There is no separate Participation grade in this class. I hope that you’ll fully engage in the discussions and activities that will take place both in person and on Canvas, to the best of your ability—which can include sharing your perspective on the topic at hand, actively listening, responding to peers verbally or in writing, etc. You will likely have a better learning experience if you actively contribute to class discussions, both in person and online. You can complete all graded coursework on Canvas.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Extra Credit

    There are no opportunities for extra credit in this class. If you become concerned about your grade, please contact me so we can discuss the best strategies for helping you succeed within the structures of the class.
